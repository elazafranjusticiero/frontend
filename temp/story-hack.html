    • Jeremy Hunt

      Secretary of State for Health

      The forthcoming report is a massive opportunity to create a better, safer NHS. The government deserves credit for holding the public inquiry, which was stubbornly refused by its predecessor. However, for its potential to be realised, Jeremy Hunt will have to put patient safety before politics and commit to implementing the recommendations in full. Not to do so would be a betrayal of patients and the NHS itself.

    • Julie Bailey

      Founder of Cure The NHS

      Julie Bailey is the founder of Cure The NHS, a patient campaign group formed to highlight the poor care at Stafford Hospital. She set up the group soon after her mother Bella's death at the hospital in November 2007

    • David Nicholson

      Chief Executive of the National Health Service

      When the long-awaited Francis report on why health service managers and regulators failed to spot the terrible failures of care at Mid Staffordshire hospital trust is published on Wednesday, the media and political spotlight will home in on what it says about the actions of one individual in particular: Sir David Nicholson.

    • Robert Francis QC

      Barrister, and report author

      Robert Francis, chair of the inquiry, is a QC specialising in the NHS and medical negligence. He has spent years examining the causes of the Stafford hospital scandal and, to quote our health correspondent Denis Campbell's profile of him, is "highly skilled at getting to the truth and quietly scathing when he feels censure is due."

    • Martin Yeates

      Hospital trust chief executive

      Martin Yeates led Mid Staffordshire Hospital Foundation trust for four years, until an investigation uncovered up to 1,200 "excess" deaths amid an appalling catalogue of failings in care. He walked away with more than £400,000 after resigning in 2009, then said he was too ill to be cross-examined over the scandal at a public inquiry, which is due to report to ministers in January.

    • Andy Burnham

      Former Labour Health Secretary

      Mr Burnham was a junior minister in the Department of Health in 2007, right in the middle of the 2005-2008 period on which the Mid Staffs report will focus.

      Mr Burnham backed Mid Staffordshire Foundation Trust's bid for foundation trust status after looking at just four lines of civil service advice, a public inquiry into the scandal heard in 2011.

      Mr Burnham's decision meant the two hospitals that made up the Mid-Staffs Foundation Trust moved further away from scrutiny as they became semi-independent of the Department of Health, leading to a further deterioration in patient care.

      He is currently Shadow Health Secretary.
